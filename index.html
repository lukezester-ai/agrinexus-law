Practical example · two models, one task

Task: Harvest is two weeks away and cash is tight. Do you prioritise locking a price now or waiting for basis to improve?

Pick a lens (switch anytime):

Why A can feel right: It protects sleep and cash-flow panic. The risk is selling into the weakest emotional window. If you choose A, your next step is to write two numbers you will not trade without: break-even €/t and minimum acceptable bid.
Why B can feel right: It forces the market structure into the story. The risk is overestimating basis recovery while bills are due. If you choose B, your next step is to estimate days-of-cash-left and the basis gap vs last season at the same calendar week.
Convince yourself

After you pick, read the short note that appears—then ask whether the other model would change your first action. No scoreboard; the habit is toggling lenses before you commit.
